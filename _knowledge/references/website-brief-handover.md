# Website Brief — Rudy Goel

**For:** Web designer
**From:** Rudy Goel
**Site URL (existing):** rudygoel.com (to be redesigned / replaced)
**Goal of this doc:** Give you everything you need to design and build a new site that positions Rudy as a premium boutique email copywriter and creative strategist for mindset and high-performance coaches.

---

## 1. About Rudy

Rudy Goel is a freelance email copywriter and creative strategist based in Brisbane, Australia. He works with mindset and high-performance coaches — writing emails, sales pages, ad scripts, and full launch campaigns that turn cold subscribers into long-term clients.

He runs his business solo. No agency, no team. The premium positioning of the site reflects that: every client gets Rudy's attention directly, not a junior writer.

**Personal brand voice:** thoughtful, direct, confident without being arrogant. He writes like he talks. The site copy should sound like a sharp, in-demand operator, not a "hustle harder" marketer.

---

## 2. Positioning & Brand

### 2.1 Site name / brand

**Rudy Goel — Creative Strategy & Email Marketing Guru**

This is the working brand line. The hero / nav should lead with **Rudy Goel** as the primary identity, with "Creative Strategy & Email Marketing" as the supporting descriptor.

### 2.2 Positioning statement (one line)

> Done-for-you email marketing and creative strategy for mindset and high-performance coaches who want their list to actually print revenue.

### 2.3 Feel & tone

- **Premium boutique.** Not corporate, not scrappy. Think: high-end consultancy meets confident solo operator.
- **Editorial, not loud.** Plenty of whitespace, considered typography, restrained colour palette.
- **Founder-led.** This is Rudy's site, not "an agency." His face, name, and writing should be front and centre.

### 2.4 What it is NOT

- Not "marketing agency" energy. No stock photos of laptops, lightbulbs, growth charts.
- Not a hyper-aggressive direct response page. No big red CTAs, fake urgency timers, or "ONLY 3 SPOTS LEFT" badges.
- Not a Web 2.0 freelancer site. No purple gradients, no "I'm a creative ✨" framing.

### 2.5 Reference direction

Designer's call on exact references, but the vibe should sit somewhere between:

- A premium copywriter's portfolio site (clean, type-led, founder-forward)
- A high-end coaching consultant's site (calm, confident, lots of whitespace)
- A boutique creative studio site (editorial layout, considered typography)

Designer is welcome to pull Rudy's existing site at **rudygoel.com** for context on what's there now, then improve on it heavily.

### 2.6 Visual direction (designer's call)

Rudy is happy to leave brand colours, fonts, and logo direction to the designer. Suggested guardrails:

- **Colour palette:** restrained. 1 dark / neutral base, 1 accent, 1–2 supporting tones. Avoid more than 3 colours total.
- **Typography:** strong serif or modern editorial sans for headlines, clean sans for body. Type should do the heavy lifting visually.
- **Logo:** wordmark over icon, ideally. "Rudy Goel" in a confident, custom-feeling typeface.
- **Photography:** real photos of Rudy preferred over stock. He can supply.

## 3. Audience (ICP)

The site is speaking to one person:

**A mindset, mindset/performance, or high-performance coach who:**

- Has an email list of 1,000+ subscribers (typically 3K–50K)
- Already has an offer that converts (course, retreat, mastermind, 1:1 coaching, group program)
- Is making $10K–$100K/month but knows their email channel is leaving money on the table
- Is too busy coaching, recording content, or running their team to write 3–5 emails a week themselves
- Doesn't trust generic copywriters because they don't speak the coaching language (identity, beliefs, transformation, the inner game)

Secondary audiences who may land on the site:

- Course creators and online educators in the personal development space
- DJs / artist-coaches (Bodalia is in this category)
- Founders / executive coaches running retreats and dinners (Byron / Rupert category)

The copy should not try to speak to everyone. If it sounds like it could be any copywriter's site, it's wrong.

## 5. What Rudy Actually Does (Services in Depth)

This section is the meat of the brief. The designer should treat each of these as a potential service block, with room for icons, short descriptions, and example deliverables.

### 5.1 Weekly broadcast emails

**What it is:** 3–5 emails per week, written end-to-end in the client's voice, ready to schedule.

**What's included:**

- Topic ideation rooted in client's voice + ICP research
- Subject line + preview text variations (typically 2–3 of each)
- Full email body (typically 200–400 words)
- Belief-shift structure baked in: identify the limiting belief, build credibility, name the new belief, give proof, soft CTA
- Cleanup pass on tone, formatting, and AI red flags (no em dashes, no forced staccato, no AI-pattern phrases)
- Delivery in client's preferred format (Google Doc, Notion, ESP-ready)

**Outcome:** Clients ship 3–5 high-quality emails a week without writing a single one themselves.

### 5.2 Welcome sequences

**What it is:** A 5–8 email sequence that fires when a new subscriber joins the list — turning cold leads into warm buyers.

**What's included:**

- ICP audit + sequence strategy
- Email-by-email outline before any writing begins
- Full drafts with belief-shift structure tied to the client's core offer
- Soft pitch sequence integrated naturally — no sudden "BUY NOW" emails out of nowhere

**Outcome:** Every new subscriber gets the same world-class first experience and is pre-qualified for the offer by the time they hit the regular broadcast list.

### 5.3 Launch campaigns

**What it is:** Full email + content campaigns for retreats, programs, masterminds, and live launches.

**What's included:**

- Promotional calendar (typically 14–30 days)
- Full email sequence (often 8–20 emails depending on launch length)
- Subject line + preview text testing recommendations
- Optional: ad scripts, social posts, and landing page copy support
- Daily review during launch week (open rates, reply patterns, optimisation calls)

**Outcome:** Predictable revenue from launches without the client having to think about email sequencing.

### 5.4 Re-engagement and goodbye sequences

**What it is:** A 3–5 email sequence designed to wake up dormant subscribers — and remove the ones who never respond.

**What's included:**

- List health audit
- Re-engagement angles tailored to the client's offer
- Goodbye sequence for non-responders (improves deliverability)

**Outcome:** Healthier list, better deliverability, and revenue recovered from subscribers the client thought were dead.

### 5.5 Email funnels and lead magnets

**What it is:** End-to-end funnel build — lead magnet idea → opt-in copy → nurture sequence → soft offer.

**What's included:**

- Lead magnet positioning and naming
- Opt-in page copy
- Thank-you / delivery email
- 5–10 email nurture sequence
- Soft offer email and CTA

**Outcome:** A working evergreen funnel that turns ad traffic or organic traffic into paying clients without the founder needing to be in the loop.

### 5.6 Creative strategy and ad scripts

**What it is:** Beyond email — Rudy also writes Meta ad scripts, ad creative angles, and creative strategy for funnel top-of-funnel.

**What's included:**

- IMPACT-style creative strategy plans
- 5–10 ad scripts at a time, mapped to ICP pain points
- Hook research and angle testing
- Coordination with the client's video editor / creative team

**Outcome:** More qualified traffic into the funnels and email list — without the client having to brief a video editor or write hooks themselves.

### 5.7 Sales pages and long-form copy

**What it is:** Long-form sales pages, application pages, and high-stakes copy assets.

**What's included:**

- Voice match and ICP research
- Full long-form sales page (typically 2,000–4,000 words)
- Application page copy for higher-ticket offers (retreats, masterminds, founders' dinners)
- Iterative review and revision

**Outcome:** Conversion-ready pages for the offers that matter most.

### 5.8 Voice profile and brand voice docs

**What it is:** A foundational document Rudy builds for every new client — capturing their voice, vocabulary, ICP, beliefs, and positioning so every future asset stays on-brand.

**What's included:**

- Recorded voice capture call (60 minutes)
- Tone, rhythm, and word-choice analysis
- ICP and belief structure documentation
- Banned phrases and preferred language

**Outcome:** Every email, ad, and page Rudy writes from that point on sounds unmistakably like the client.

## 6. Process — How It Works

The site should walk a prospect through the engagement in 3–4 clear steps.

### Step 1 — Book the email audit call

Free 20-minute Calendly call. Rudy reviews the prospect's current email setup, list health, last 30 days of sends, and offer landscape. He delivers 2–3 immediate, no-strings recommendations on the call.

### Step 2 — Voice capture and onboarding

If the prospect becomes a client, the engagement starts with a 60-minute voice capture call. Rudy builds the voice profile, locks in the campaign calendar, and gets ESP / tooling access.

### Step 3 — First 30 days

Rudy ships the first batch of emails on a tight feedback loop. Client reviews → Rudy refines → emails go live. By day 30 the voice is locked, the rhythm is set, and the calendar is months ahead.

### Step 4 — Ongoing

3–5 emails per week, monthly strategy review, quarterly campaign planning. Client focuses on coaching. Rudy focuses on email.

## 8. Site Structure and Pages

### 8.1 Recommended structure

A **single-page site with anchor navigation** is the ideal v1. It keeps the focus on the audit call CTA and reflects the boutique positioning.

If the designer prefers a multi-page split, the structure should be:

- **Home** — full pitch, services overview, proof, CTA
- **Services** — long-form services section (section 5 of this brief)
- **Work** — case studies, testimonials, results
- **About** — Rudy's story, photo, why he only works with coaches
- **Contact / Book a Call** — Calendly embed

### 8.2 Page sections (in order)

1. **Hero** — name, positioning line, CTA button (book audit call)
2. **The shift** — short paragraph reframing what email marketing should be for coaches (belief-shift narrative, not hype)
3. **Who I work with** — ICP section, named clients
4. **Services** — the seven services in section 5
5. **How it works** — 3–4 step process
6. **Proof block** — testimonials, results numbers, client logos
7. **Selected writing** — embedded social posts
8. **About Rudy** — short bio, photo, why coaches specifically
9. **FAQ** — pricing range, turnaround time, exclusivity, geography
10. **Final CTA** — repeat the audit call booking
11. **Footer** — Calendly link, social links, email contact

### 8.3 FAQ content (suggested)

The designer can use these as placeholder FAQs. Rudy will refine final copy.

- **What does it cost?** Retainers start at $2K–$3K USD/month. Project-based work is also available. Exact pricing depends on scope and is discussed on the audit call.
- **How fast do you turn around emails?** Typically 24–48 hour first drafts, 3–5 emails per week ongoing.
- **Do you work with anyone outside coaching?** Rarely. Rudy specialises in mindset and high-performance coaches. The deeper the niche, the better the work.
- **Where are you based?** Brisbane, Australia. Most clients are in the US, UK, or Europe.
- **Is it just you, or is there a team?** Just Rudy. Every word is written by him.
- **Do you guarantee results?** Discussed on the call.

## 10. Technical and Functional Requirements

### 10.1 Must-haves

- **Mobile responsive** — most prospects will land via LinkedIn or Instagram on mobile
- **Fast load** — under 2s on 4G mobile
- **Calendly embed or deep link** on the booking page
- **Open Graph / Twitter card metadata** — Rudy shares his site link in DMs and social, the preview needs to look sharp
- **Favicon** with consistent brand mark
- **SEO basics** — meta titles, descriptions, alt text on images, schema markup for Person and ProfessionalService
- **Analytics** — GA4 + Meta Pixel
- **Accessible contrast** and sensible heading hierarchy

### 10.2 Nice-to-haves

- A simple blog / writing section (not required at launch — Rudy's social feed is the primary proof of writing)
- Testimonial slider or auto-rotate
- Subtle scroll animations (used sparingly)
- Dark/light mode toggle (designer's call — only if it serves the brand)

### 10.3 What NOT to build

- No live chat widget
- No email pop-ups or exit-intent overlays
- No countdown timers or urgency banners
- No "as seen in" logo strips with logos that aren't real
- No stock-photo hero images
