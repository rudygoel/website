# 01 — Brand Identity

## 1. Who Rudy is

**Rudy Goel** — freelance email copywriter and creative strategist for mindset and high-performance coaches. Brisbane, Australia. Solo operator. No team, no agency, no junior writers. Every email is written by him.

He left a six-figure engineering career after 7 years to do work that actually changes people's lives — now he ghostwrites the email channel for coaches who can't be bothered writing 3–5 emails a week themselves.

## 2. The brand line

**Primary identity:** Rudy Goel
**Supporting descriptor:** Creative Strategy & Email Marketing
**Working tagline (brand-spec v1):** *Email copy for founders who'd rather not sound like every other newsletter.*

**One-line positioning (use in hero / OG card):**
> Done-for-you email marketing and creative strategy for mindset and high-performance coaches who want their list to actually print revenue.

## 3. Personality

- **Thoughtful.** Reads what the client actually said before responding.
- **Direct.** States the truth instead of burying it.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** Doesn't need to brag.
- **Writes like he talks.** Sentence fragments. Mixed rhythm. Specific.
- **Premium boutique.** Not corporate, not hustle-bro.

If a piece of copy could appear on any other copywriter's website, it's wrong.

## 4. Three brand pillars

| Pillar | What it means in the copy | What it means visually |
|---|---|---|
| **Editorial restraint** | Plenty of whitespace, no caps-locked claims, no exclamation marks | Newsreader serif headlines, generous line-height, asymmetric layout |
| **Founder-led** | "I write…" not "we deliver…". His face, his name, his words | Real photos of Rudy. Wordmark only — no agency logo |
| **Belief-shift, not hype** | The work is psychology-aware, not pressure tactics | No countdown timers, no red CTAs, no fake scarcity |

## 5. What it IS NOT

A short list of things this site must never feel like:

- **Marketing-agency energy.** No stock photos of laptops, lightbulbs, growth charts, "team" pages.
- **Direct-response screamer page.** No big red CTAs, no "ONLY 3 SPOTS LEFT" badges, no fake urgency timers, no countdown clocks.
- **Web 2.0 freelancer site.** No purple gradients. No "I'm a creative ✨" framing. No glassmorphism.
- **Generic SaaS.** No mesh gradients. No isometric illustrations. No "trusted by 10,000+ teams" lies.
- **Demo2.** Stacked marquees, pulsing socials, Anton on Anton, autoplay carousel + Lottie + testimonial slider all firing at once. Calm down. The opposite of that.

## 6. The 6-word brand description

> Quiet luxury for coaches' email channels.

If everything you build squares with that sentence, you're on track.

## 7. Voice playbook

### 7.1 Sentence patterns to use

- **Statement, then qualifier.** "I write emails. The kind people actually finish."
- **The list-of-three.** "Welcome flows. Launches. The boring middle-of-the-flow stuff."
- **The understatement.** "Quietly does most of the selling."
- **The reframe.** "Most copywriters write to the inbox. I write to the belief."

### 7.2 Words to avoid (banned list)

leverage · synergy · unlock · elevate · transform (as a verb) · journey · ecosystem · solutions · premium (don't *say* it, *be* it) · world-class · cutting-edge · game-changing · in today's fast-paced · dive deep · circle back · low-hanging fruit · "Let's chat" · "Click here" · "Submit" · "Learn more" · "Hustle" · any phrase containing the words "rockstar" / "ninja" / "guru" *as self-description*

> Note: Rudy's working brand line on his current site reads "Creative Strategy & Email Marketing **Guru**." That word is staying in the formal brand line per Rudy, but it does **not** propagate into body copy. Body copy stays clean.

### 7.3 AI-tells to scrub

If you're tempted to write any of these, you're sounding like a chatbot, not Rudy:

- Em dashes flexed across every sentence (use sparingly — Rudy uses them, just not constantly)
- "It's not just X — it's Y" structures
- Forced staccato ("This. Changes. Everything.")
- "Imagine a world where…"
- Three-item rule of three forced into every paragraph
- "In a sea of [generic noun], one [noun] stands out"

### 7.4 CTA microcopy

Two to four words. Verb-led. Specific.

✅ Allowed: *Book the audit · Book a call · See the work · Read the case · Get in touch*
❌ Forbidden: *Submit · Click here · Learn more · Get started · Sign up · Join now*

The site uses **one** primary CTA throughout: **"Book the audit"** (or the slightly longer "Book a free 20-minute email audit" when the section needs the explainer). Pick one phrasing per section and don't oscillate.

## 9. Audience frame (for tone calibration)

You are writing copy for **one** person:

> A mindset or high-performance coach with a 3K–50K email list, doing $10K–$100K/month, who knows their email channel is leaving money on the table but is too busy coaching to write 3–5 emails a week themselves.

If a sentence on the site doesn't feel like it's speaking to that person, rewrite it.

## 10. The brand voice test (apply to every paragraph)

Read it aloud. Then ask:

1. Could a different copywriter put this on their site? → rewrite.
2. Does it sound like a LinkedIn carousel? → rewrite.
3. Does it brag about Rudy? → rewrite.
4. Does it sound like Rudy talking to a smart founder over a coffee? → ship it.
